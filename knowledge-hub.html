Climate-Smart Agriculture

Climate-smart agriculture (CSA) refers to farming practices that increase productivity, resilience, and environmental sustainability while reducing greenhouse gas emissions. It emphasizes the use of adaptive techniques such as crop diversification, water-efficient irrigation, and improved soil management to combat the impacts of climate change. By promoting sustainable practices, CSA aims to enhance food security, support livelihoods, and contribute to global climate change mitigation efforts.

Precision Farming

Precision farming involves using advanced technologies like GPS, sensors, drones, and data analytics to monitor and manage field variability in crops, optimizing resource use and improving yields. It allows farmers to make data-driven decisions, tailoring inputs such as water, fertilizer, and pesticides to specific areas of a field for maximum efficiency. This approach helps reduce waste, increase productivity, and minimize environmental impact, making farming more sustainable and cost-effective.
